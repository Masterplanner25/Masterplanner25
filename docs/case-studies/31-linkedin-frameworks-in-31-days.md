## Context

## What Happened

Shawn Knight launched a 31-day mini-series: each day, he selected a LinkedIn creator, had AI analyze their most effective post, and extracted the underlying framework that made it work. The result was 31 documented content frameworks — a systematic extraction of what makes LinkedIn content effective, powered by AI analysis.

The significance is methodological: instead of guessing why content works, the series used AI to reverse-engineer the structural patterns. Each framework was documented, named, and published — creating an indexed library of AI-analyzed content mechanics.

The mini-series became a reference in the AISO context: understanding what content patterns AI search systems recognize and amplify is itself a form of canonical anchoring research.

## Outcomes

- Launched the 31 LinkedIn Frameworks in 31 Days mini-series as a structured AI analysis experiment
- Produced 31 documented, named content frameworks derived from AI-assisted pattern extraction
- Demonstrated AI as a reverse-engineering tool for content structure, not just a generation tool
