## What Happened

This article introduces the Prompt vs Presence framework through a live iteration thread — showing the development of the concept in real time rather than presenting a polished conclusion.

The framework draws a hard line between two modes of AI engagement:
- **Prompt**: a transaction — command the AI, receive output, exit
- **Presence**: a relationship — sustained engagement, accumulated context, collaborative depth

The argument: most people use AI as a prompt machine and wonder why results feel shallow. Presence — showing up repeatedly, building context, treating AI as a collaborative intelligence rather than a vending machine — produces fundamentally different (and deeper) results.

This framework became the philosophical backbone of the AISO methodology and the canonical anchoring strategy: both require presence, not prompts.
