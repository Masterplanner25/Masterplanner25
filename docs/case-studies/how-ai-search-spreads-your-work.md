## What Happened

Shawn Knight observed that consistent, semantically dense publishing across indexed platforms caused AI search systems to pick up and distribute his content without active promotion. The article documents this as evidence that the AISO strategy works: AI systems reward semantic density, consistency, and cross-platform presence rather than social engagement metrics.

The mechanism: AI search indexes what is semantically rich and consistently labeled. When a creator has built a canonical anchor with enough density, AI systems do the distribution work — spreading the work to anyone who queries a related topic.

## Outcomes

- Provided observable evidence that AI search amplification operates independently of social platform algorithms
- Connected the AISO methodology to practical distribution outcomes
- Established that canonical anchoring is not just discoverability — it is active distribution by AI systems
