## Context

A documented case study of what happens when conventional SEO/GEO experts encounter an AI-native builder whose results contradict their frameworks. This article documents the encounter with Daniel T. Sasser II and treats it not as a feud but as empirical documentation.

## Entities Involved

- **[Duality of Progress](../entities/duality-of-progress.md)** — the series this article appears in
- **[AI Search Optimization (AISO)](../entities/ai-search-optimization.md)** — the practice being challenged by the "experts"

## What Happened

When Shawn Knight's AISO results became visible — AI systems returning his work as references, external citations, documented validation events — conventional SEO and GEO practitioners pushed back. Daniel T. Sasser II was one such figure whose response to the results went beyond critique into documented pushback.

The article documents the exchange not as a fight but as data: what happens when results that don't fit the expert framework are presented to experts who built their identity on that framework. The "meltdown" is the observable response to cognitive dissonance between the expert's model and the documented reality.

The significance: expert pushback, when documented, itself becomes canonical evidence. The reaction to the results is part of the results.

## Outcomes

- Documented the expert pushback response to AISO results as empirical data
- Established that opposition from credentialed experts validates rather than undermines the methodology
- Created a reference document for how established frameworks react to results that contradict them
