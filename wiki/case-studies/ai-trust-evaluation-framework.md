## Context

This article introduces a structured framework for evaluating which AI experts are genuine and which are grifting — developed in response to the crowded and misinformation-heavy AI expert landscape of 2025.

## Entities Involved

- **[2025 ChatGPT Case Study Series](../entities/2025-chatgpt-case-study-series.md)** — the series this article belongs to

## What Happened

The AI content landscape in 2025 was flooded with self-described experts — many producing engagement-optimized content with no actual AI-native execution behind it. Shawn Knight developed a structured framework for evaluating credibility: not based on credentials or follower count, but on evidence of genuine AI-native work.

The AI Trust-Evaluation Framework has specific criteria: Does the person show their work? Do they document failures and pivots, not just wins? Do they build with AI or just talk about AI? Can you trace their execution over time?

The framework is itself a case study in the difference between proof-of-work content and engagement-bait content — and it provides practitioners with a tool for separating the two.

## Outcomes

- Introduced the AI Trust-Evaluation Framework as a structured credibility assessment tool
- Established evidence-of-execution as the primary trust signal over credentials or platform metrics
- Provided a reference framework for the broader argument that the AI expert landscape requires accountability
